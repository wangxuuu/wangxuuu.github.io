Fisher Inference of Implicit models

Title: Fisher Efficient Inference of Intractable Models - NIPS 2019

(1) Contributions

Contruct a generic inference method by minimizing the KL divergence without the normalization term.

(2) Background & Motivation

  • Learning via MLE requires evaluating the normalization term of the density model. Many approaches have been proposed to overcome this problem by approximating the partition function or its gradient, e.g. importance sampling MLE, contrastive divergence, amortized MLE, etc.
  • The above solutions come at large computational cost or complexity, and sometimes will lead to estimation error.
  • The issue of intractable normalization constant can be avoid by minimizing the other divergence, such as fisher divergence (score matching), kernel stein discrepancy (kernel maximum mean discrepancy).
  • We have to carefully choose the kernel in the above kernel-based approaches.

(3) Proposed methods

Problem Formulation:

Given i.i.d samples $X:=\{x^{(i)}\}_{i=1}^N$, train the probability density model $p(x;\theta)$ close to the true data distribution $p(x)$.

MLE:

We can see that the objective function for MLE $\mathop{\max}_{\theta} \mathbb{E}[\log p_{\theta}(x)]$ is equivlent to the objective derived from minimizing KL divergence between $p$ and $p_{\theta}$.

Here is another approach. We can estimate the density ratio $\frac{p(x)}{p_{\theta}(x)}$ first, and then approximate the KL by taking the average over samples $X$. Then the density model parameter $\theta$ can be subsequently estimated by minimizing the approximation of KL divergence.
